The College of Applied Studies and Community Service represents one of the leading colleges at King Saud University. It was founded by virtue of the university rector's decision No. 8517/2, issued on 11/10/1402 H, which provided for the establishment of KSU "Community Service Center" to serve as a link between the university and its all local community groups and sectors, including ministries, public and private institutions, and individuals, by providing courses and various programs which meet the needs of the community.

Academic Departments

Administrative And Humanities Sciences

In line with the university’s role in developing and enhancing human resources, and with a belief in the importance of this role in the success and productivity of institutions, the Department of Administrative and Humanities Sciences was established at the College of Applied Studies and Community Service at King Saud University. Its objective is to prepare well-qualified human resources that highlight the university’s role in serving the community and its dedication to advancing and developing human resources. The department also aims to cultivate the scientific and practical skills of community members. Currently, the Department of Administrative and Humanities Sciences offers six diploma programs. One of these programs, the General Diploma in Education, is directed toward university graduates, while the remaining five diplomas are designed for high school graduates. These diplomas include Accounting, Law, Insurance, Banking, and Marketing. The General Diploma in Education aims to prepare teachers for various educational stages with a solid educational foundation. The other diplomas aim to supply the labor market with qualified and trained workforce ready to join immediately. Additionally, the department occasionally offers other diplomas, such as the Diploma in Radio and Television Production Assistance, which introduces the principles and techniques of radio and television production, and the Diploma in Student Counseling and Guidance, which aims to prepare and qualify psychological specialists to guide students in primary, middle, and secondary schools based on the objectives and tasks outlined by the Ministry of Education.

Vision

To achieve leadership and excellence in applied education and community service within administrative and humanities sciences programs locally and globally.

Mission

We aim to build bridges of cooperation between the college and its graduates, exchange knowledge and experiences with them, strengthen their affiliation with the college, encourage their contributions to its service, and support them in achieving the desired goals and excellence.

Objectives

  • Provide opportunities for students who did not join the university to complete their applied and academic education and earn intermediate diplomas in fields required by the labor market.
  • Deliver high-quality applied education capable of graduating students with competitive scientific knowledge and practical skills.
  • Develop curricula based on diverse learning and teaching methods that align with the needs of students and the business sector.
  • Enhance various assessment strategies and tools to measure the diverse aspects of learners and improve the educational process.
  • Enable students to acquire knowledge and apply it professionally and ethically.
  • Continuously develop and update departments to support applied education.
  • Encourage administrative and humanities research for faculty members through partnerships with other university disciplines and local and global collaborations.
  • Host specialized scientific and professional events to advance administrative education, research, and humanities sciences.
  • Attract distinguished academic talent locally and globally to support the scientific journey

Computer Science And Engineering

The Department of Natural and Engineering Sciences aims to elevate the academic level of its programs to meet the highest standards expected in the professional field, focusing on a strong educational structure and distinguished teaching staff. The department integrates both theoretical and practical components to allow students to undertake various projects throughout their studies, preparing them for practical and technical tasks related to their coursework and aligned with job market demands. The department also provides students with opportunities for summer internships at public and private institutions to refine their skills and prepare them for the workforce. With the increasing importance of fields like telecommunications, computer networks, databases, and cybersecurity due to the notable growth in tasks and operations within different sectors and the need for local and international connectivity, the Department of Natural and Engineering Sciences has introduced the Diploma in Telecommunications and Computer Networks, the Diploma in Programming and Databases, and the Bachelor’s in Applied Computing with tracks in Computer Networks and Cybersecurity. These programs are designed to meet job market requirements, equipping students with the necessary skills and knowledge for network operation, installation, maintenance, management, and security, as well as ensuring the security of internet-provided services. The Diploma in Programming and Databases is crafted to develop students’ programming abilities and train them in modern programming methods through intensive practical applications, along with training in database design, maintenance, and handling various systems.

Vision

To bridge the gap between theory and practice and position the Department of Natural and Engineering Sciences as a model of excellence in applied education.

Mission

To deliver high-quality programs in natural and engineering sciences, implementing the highest standards in applied education to prepare qualified professionals for the job market.

Objectives

  • Provide qualified human resources in natural and engineering sciences for technical roles in the public and private sectors.
  • Offer diverse programs in natural and engineering sciences, including continuing education and short- and long-term training courses for the community.
  • Establish and strengthen partnerships with the private sector to align the college’s output with the practical requirements of available jobs

Health Sciences

The Department of Health Sciences offers five diploma programs:

  1. Radiologic Diagnosis Diploma - This five-semester program teaches students various types of radiologic imaging under the supervision of qualified professors and technicians, enabling graduates to work as skilled radiology technicians.
  2. Medical Laboratory Diploma - Also spanning five semesters, this program provides students with knowledge of laboratory testing principles and offers training in university hospitals and other medical institutions.
  3. Medical Records Diploma - This five-semester program covers the basics of medical file management, focusing on electronic handling and computer skills in various study areas.
  4. Medical Secretary Diploma - This diploma prepares graduates for a distinguished career in medical secretarial work, with comprehensive training throughout the study period.
  5. Nursing Diploma - Exclusively for female students, this program prepares graduates to be skilled nurses capable of patient care and management in major hospitals through rigorous studies and intensive training over five semesters.

However, due to the recent decision by the Minister of Health to discontinue all intermediate diplomas in health specializations, all of the above diplomas have been suspended, except for the Medical Secretary Diploma for both male and female students. Several other health-related programs are under consideration for addition to the Department of Health Sciences. The department is committed to developing and updating its programs to meet the needs of the labor market and society, aiming to achieve its objectives with dedication and purpose.

Mission

To maintain quality in specializations and excellence in academic and educational performance, enhancing the graduates’ abilities and preparing them to compete in the job market

Non-Degree Course Enrollment Program

About the Program This educational program allows members of the community to study one or more courses from King Saud University without being regular students. The aim is to enable individuals to acquire educational and knowledge-based experiences in areas that meet their needs and interests.

Beneficiaries

  • ndividuals interested in gaining expertise or improving specific academic and scientific skills without committing to a degree-granting program.
  • Undergraduate or graduate students who wish to take specific courses to fulfill graduate program admission requirements or to gain knowledge in a research-related field, such as statistics.
  • Individuals preparing for exams or completing requirements for professional certification.
  • Visiting students enrolled in a bachelor’s program at another college or university.
  • Those interested in expanding their knowledge in various fields of science and humanities

Non-Degree Course Enrollment Guidelines

  • The applicant’s qualifications or practical experience must meet the academic requirements for the desired course.
  • Admission may require additional conditions or passing a personal interview.
  • Completing these courses does not grant admission to the university’s academic programs.
  • Enrollment in specialized courses, such as fieldwork or research-based, clinical, or therapeutic courses, requires approval from the relevant college.
  • Beneficiaries can study up to 23 credit hours at the diploma and bachelor’s levels and 12 credit hours at the graduate level.
  • Students enrolled in non-degree courses are treated as regular students and must meet all course requirements, including attendance, participation, assignments, and exams. They will receive an academic transcript with their final grade at the end of the semester.
  • In cases of withdrawal or course drop, the refund policy applied to students of the College of Applied Studies and Community Service will be enforced
